Doctor Opinions

The Importance Of Early Detection And Awareness

Dr. Sumeet Shah 12 April, 2020

Why are we discussing breast cancer awareness?

Because, breast cancer is the most common cancer in women in India, both, in cities, as well as in rural areas and accounts for almost 25 to 33% of all cancers. The number of women being detected every year with breast cancer in India actually is much lesser, as compared to countries like US, China and European countries. But the number of women dying from breast cancer in India is highest in the world.

Why is there such a high death rate?

Because most of the women come in a ‘late’ stage, where the cancer has already spread beyond the breast.

Why do most women come at a late stage?

Now, the most common symptom of a breast cancer is a ‘painless’ lump in the breast. If patients reach doctor within a few weeks of noticing this lump, it is usually an early stage and they get cured. But, many women do not pay attention to it because it is ‘painless’ and not causing any problem, or maybe some of them have a doubt, but they ignore it. Most of the women do not ‘know’ that this ‘lump’ could be a cancer – they are not ‘aware’, they do not know the ‘symptoms’ of a breast cancer. Only when the lump increases to a big size or spreads and they start developing problems, they come to a doctor. When I ask my patients, that had they known this could be cancerous, would they have come earlier? And most would say yes to that.

What's the takeaway?

So, the moral of the story is ‘awareness’. As a doctor, I just want women to know what are the symptoms of breast cancer – they are barely a few – a ‘lump’ or a ‘knot’ in the breast which feels very ‘hard’, a ‘lumpish’ feel, blood or blood-stained nipple discharge, ‘inward’ pulling of the nipple, thickening of the skin over the breast, knots in the armpit (See the link below for symptoms). I just want them to keep these symptoms in mind, and do a self-examination of the breast once in a month, around the fifth to the seventh day of the cycle (after periods). This is called ‘Breast Awareness’. If women do not do any tests, I am fine. But if they sit at home with a lump for six months, I am not fine. I want them to reach their doctor within a few weeks of them noticing a lump. As simple as that. Also, all lumps are not cancerous. In young, such lumps ae fibroids, and do not become cancer. As age increases above 30, chances of them being cancerous also increases. So, any lump, it’s best to seek a medical opinion. Early detection of cancer will definitely increase cure, and lead to lesser numbers of women dying from it. Remember, if a cancer has to happen, it will happen. We can’t change that. But if we detect cancer on time, we can be cured, and we CAN reduce the deaths due to it. That’s what most western countries have achieved and we can do it too.
